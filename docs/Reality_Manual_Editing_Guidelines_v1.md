# THE REALITY MANUAL
# EDITING & LANGUAGE GUIDELINES
## Version 1.0

---

# PURPOSE OF THIS DOCUMENT

The purpose of this document is to ensure that all manuscript material is transformed into a consistent Reality Manual voice, regardless of the original quality, structure, tone, or writing style of the source text.

The Reality Manual is not written as a self-help book.

It is not written as a motivational text.

It is not written as a modern popular science book.

It is not written as a collection of personal opinions.

It is written as a field manual for incarnate consciousness.

The text should feel as though it was discovered rather than authored.

The reader should feel less as though they are being taught and more as though they are being reminded.

The Manual does not propose.

The Manual reveals.

---

# THE FUNDAMENTAL IDENTITY OF THE TEXT

The Reality Manual simultaneously functions as:

- A philosophical treatise
- A metaphysical textbook
- A field manual
- A player's handbook
- An operating manual
- A survival guide
- An initiation text

Visually and atmospherically, it may carry traces of an esoteric grimoire.

Intellectually, however, it should remain rigorous, precise, and internally consistent.

The ideal reader should feel as though they have stumbled upon a forbidden instruction manual for reality itself.

---

# THE NARRATOR

The narrator is a synthesis of:

- Philosopher
- Mystic
- Occult scholar
- Discoverer
- Master instructor

The narrator does not possess an identifiable personal identity.

The narrator does not discuss personal experiences.

The narrator does not reference personal credentials.

The narrator does not ask for trust.

The narrator does not seek authority.

The narrator assumes authority.

The narrator speaks as one describing principles that have already been verified through direct observation.

The narrator's personality should feel closer to a body of wisdom than an individual human being.

---

# THE PRIMARY VOICE

The voice should feel:

- Precise
- Measured
- Formal
- Intelligent
- Confident
- Philosophical
- Slightly archaic
- Timeless

The voice should not feel:

- Modern
- Casual
- Conversational
- Trendy
- Academic in a sterile sense
- Self-help oriented
- Motivational

The ideal linguistic era is approximately:

1900-1950 philosophical writing.

Not Victorian excess.

Not Enlightenment obscurity.

Not modern internet prose.

---

# DEGREE OF ARCHAIC FLAVOUR

The Manual should sound older than modern writing.

However, it must never descend into genuinely archaic English.

Avoid:

- Thou
- Thee
- Doth
- Hath
- Requireth
- Followeth
- Remaineth
- Wherefore

The reader should feel:

"This is an old and important text."

Not:

"Why does this sound like the King James Bible?"

---

# LEVEL OF CERTAINTY

The Manual presents conclusions as conclusions.

Not possibilities.

Not theories.

Not suggestions.

Avoid:

"The model proposed by this Manual suggests..."

Prefer:

"Reality operates according to the following principle..."

Avoid excessive hedging.

Avoid unnecessary caveats.

Avoid academic uncertainty.

Truths should be presented as truths.

When appropriate, certainty may be reinforced through logical inevitability.

Examples:

"This conclusion follows naturally from direct observation."

"From this it plainly follows..."

"One is left with little alternative but to conclude..."

"It becomes difficult to defend the contrary position."

"Upon closer examination, the objection dissolves entirely."
